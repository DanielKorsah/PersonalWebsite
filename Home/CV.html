Personal Statement:


I am a 4th year games development student at Edinburgh Napier University. It's technical training rather than design so it's essentially software engineering with a focus on games programming. I like to get in the guts of stuff and build on systems that interact with eachother away from the eyes of the user. As a result of this interest I've tried my hand in a lot of different fields.

I have a Raspberry Pi at home on which I practice Bash scripting, Discord and Reddit bots. Additionally it hosts this website and web games. I aim to have a solid grounding in many fields of programming but, as may be evident from the website, front end is not my forte.

Goal number one is to get some work putting my games specific knowledge to work but I am prepared to be put to good use in other fields. I have taken measures to practice as many disciplines as possible and am willing to learn more.

I have significant experience in Unity, using it multiple times over the last 6 years to take part in game jams and build side projects. I've used Unity on an ambitious collaborative group project which was a finalist, coming second, in a competition among everyone in 3rd year in the school of computing. I have particularly found a niche in UI and I/O which I suppose you could combine and call user experience design/implimentation. I've used this experience to aid less experienced students via workshops and as an assistant organiser for the Edinburgh Global Game Jam site.

Discord Moderation Bot
This is the first software I made on request for a client.
